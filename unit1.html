1.1 - The purpose of IT in modern business:

Companies can use IT to make business a lot easier than it was a decade ago, they can
do this by using any of these methods:
  • Communication: Companies can us IT to Email, VoIP (Voice of Internet Protocol), live
    chat, online meeting tools, and video conference systems to easily share files and talk to
    each other with ease, even if they aren’t in the same building or even the same country.
    Using these they can also communicate with customers.

  • Inventory: Inventory and stock management can be done with the use of IT, companies
    can easily see how much stock they have left, and easily communicate with providers to
    order more if needed, they can also see what is selling quickly and make arrangements
    to create a sale of bulk items.

  • Data Management: Thanks to modern IT development companies can store 100,000s of
    documents without filling a room with dozens of filing cabinets, they can then with a
    simple click of a button access any document they need, or search for any archived data.

  • Management Information Systems (MIS): MIS enables companies to manage all the data
    stored and monitor sales data, expenses, and productivity. The data stored can be used
    to maximise investment returns, profits and areas that require improvement. Companies
    can use the data to see sales to track lower than expected numbers in the sale of items,
    and make amends by either boosting staff productivity or reducing the price of items.

  • Customer Relationship Management (CRM): CRM is beneficial to businesses since it
    makes helping customers much simpler, now if a customer calls with an issue with a
    product, the customer support service can easily bring up all the data about the order
    including the item, shipping information, and can bring up any documents that came with
    the item such as an instruction manual, and assist the customer efficiently, or if needed
    help with returning the order and issuing a refund or replacement.

1.2 - Product Management:

In order to make a product successfully a product manager needs to use a number of
skills and methods to be able to gain the most productivity and create a product with little
to no issues. Some of the things a product manager will need to think about are:

  • Creating a plan of the project and sharing it with the other people they’re working with.

  • Set a budget on how much they are wanting to spend on the project

  • Set a deadline of when they want the project completed.

  • Assigning tasks to the correct people who specialise in that area to maximise productivity.

  • Scheduling check-ins to allow everyone know what's been done, what needs doing and keep up on progress of the project

  • Escalating challenges when they’re needed, so if something needs doing quickly they need to bring people together and get them done.


On top of thinking about all of this a product manager also needs skills that can help
maximise productivity and help push a project out quickly and efficiently, these skills are:

  • Collaboration - They need to be able to work with others to successfully complete a project

  • Communication - They need to be able to communicate with people clearly, since if they fail to communicate
    then no one will know what to do or who’s doing what.

  • Problem solving - They should be able to solve problems

  • Organization - They need to be organized since a cluttered work space will lead no many issues and loss of information

  • Multi-task - Multi tasking is a must, they need to be able to do more than 1 thing at a time,
    since they could be asked to do something else quickly while working on something else
    that is important


Something that product managers will need to always use is the Management Tools, this
is so they can easily monitor progress, and amend any issues that arise such as a key
project member being away, the manager can use the tools to check to see if they can
afford the time to wait until the member is back to finish the task, then change the
deadline dates and make everyone aware of the change, or if they can’t afford the time
they can reassign the task to another person that has either completed the task they’re
on, or have no current project to work on.

1.4 - What could cause Projects to Fail:

There are many things that could cause projects to fail, it’s up to the Project Manager to
try and minimise or eliminate these if possible. But sometimes these issues are
completely unavoidable and can cause major problems that could either halt progress or
completely shut a project down. The issues could be any of the following:

  • Human Error - an employee could accidentally delete something or miss click somewhere and,
    cause issues which need to be solved ASAP, they could prevent this by saving the files regularly.

  • System Glitches - even though computers are getting more advanced by the day, they
    aren’t completely safe from problems, at any point a glitch could occur that could cause a
    computer to crash, causing major issues, and worse than this it could cause data
    corruption, which would cause a catastrophic problem if the data doesn’t have previous
    save points that the user can load up and re-do the parts that haven’t saved.

  • Poor Management - if a Manager doesn’t do what they are supposed to do, then the
    employees won’t be able to work with full productivity and miss deadlines, or not
    complete essential tasks.

1.7 - How does GDPR affect Projects

EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) effects Project Management and
development in many ways. Due to the regulations in place, people have the right to
request all data stored about them, they also have the right to object it being stored, or
have it all completely erased, unless it needs to be kept for certain reasons. These can
can cause projects to be delayed, since a small project team need to be certain they can
follow these regulations before starting a project. With GDPR data storage companies
but notify everyone on their system if a data breach occurs, and alert them to the
possibility that there stored data may now be accessible to hackers, it is up to the data
storage company to do everything they can to keep all data stored, as secure as possible.
